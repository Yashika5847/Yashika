Ratan Tata

Biography

Tata was born in Bombay, into Parsi family, on 28 th December 1937. He studied at the Campion School, Mumbai till the 8th standard. After which, he graduated in 1955. After graduation, Tata enrolled in Cornell University, from which he graduated with bachelor degree in architecture in 1959.

During the 21 years Tata led the Tata Group, revenues grew over 40 times, and profit over 50 times.

Ratan Tata received the Padma Bhushan in 2000 and Padma Vibhushan in 2008, the third and second highest civilian honours awarded by the Government of India.

Tata is supporter of education, medicine and rural development, and considered leading philanthropist in India. Tata supported University of New South Wales Faculty of Engineering to develop capacitive deionization to provide improved water for challenged areas.
